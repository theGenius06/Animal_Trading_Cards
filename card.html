The Lynx

Lynx are skilled hunters due to their great hearing and eyesight so strong that they can spot a mouse 250 feet away! They are creative, and can build shelters under fallen trees or rock edges.

  • Scientific Name: Lynx Pardinus
  • Jump Distance: 23 Feet
  • Favorite Prey: Wild Rabbits
  • Top Speed: Over 50 Miles Per Hour!

The Iberian lynx (Lynx pardinus) is a medium-sized wild cat native to the Iberian Peninsula in southwestern Europe. It is one of the most endangered feline species in the world. The Iberian lynx is easily recognizable by its tufted ears, a short tail with a black tip, and a distinctive facial ruff with long whiskers.

The Black Panther

Black Panthers don't really exist! These animals are actually jaguars or leopards, with the rare occasion of black fur.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera Pardus
  • Jump Distance: 40 Feet
  • Favorite Prey: Deer
  • Top Speed: 50 Miles Per Hour

The black panther is not a separate species but rather a melanistic (dark-colored) variant of several species of large cats, primarily leopards (Panthera pardus) in Asia and Africa and jaguars (Panthera onca) in the Americas. The term "black panther" refers to these animals' dark, almost black, fur, which is caused by a genetic mutation that results in an excess of dark pigmentation. Black panthers are known for their stealth and power, making them apex predators in their respective habitats.
